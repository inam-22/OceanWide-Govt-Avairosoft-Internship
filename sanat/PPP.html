  • PPP

    Private Public Partnership In recent years, Public Private Partnerships have proven themselves as an extremely efficient form of cooperation between the public and private sectors in the execution of projects and it has, to an ever -increasing degree, established itself as an alternative to more traditional approaches to awarding contracts. Under the Private Finance Initiative, the public sector enters into long-term contracts with private sector companies to design, build, finance and operate a new facility such as a hospital or school etc. This option introduced to the government can start a significant proportion of large-scale investment projects in areas such as Health, Education, Defense, Transport, Council Housing, Energy, Water Infrastructure and Waste Management in-turn stabilizing countries. Private finance can be used as a method of increasing investment in public services, which are widely perceived to have suffered from underfunding in “post conflict” zones. The use of public private partnerships (PPPs) as they are can be projected and targeted as needed to support all Government needs and projects.

    PROCUREMENT PROCESSSPVSPECIAL PROJECT MANAGEMENT

    When undertaking a PPP, a public authority carries out a competitive procurement process, with a number of private consortia bidding against each other on the basis of price and quality to undertake the project. Each consortium involves a mix of investors, such as construction and facilities management companies and private equity institutions.

    The vehicle used for this type of program is a SPV. The SPV enters into sub-contracts with one or more firms (usually its own shareholders) to deliver the project. Finance is also raised from the shareholders, and ‘senior debt’ is raised from banks or the capital markets. In the standard SPV financial structure, senior debt will provide 90% of the finance required, with loans and equity capital from the shareholders making up the remaining 10%.

    At OWS GmbH, all requests, offers and proposals for Special Projects are processed in the Public Private Partnership department. Due to the long project periods that are connected with this special project form, often set up over a number of decades, special attention is paid to the costs for reinvestment, in addition to optimally designed operations, because these make up a significant portion of the life cycle costs.
